The Haka was an ancient Polynesian war dance. It is performed to get warriors pumped up before a fight, as well as to intimidate enemies. The bulging eyes and tongue sticking out signifies a warrior's desire to eat his enemies and absorb their Mana. Each Polynesian culture developed their own words and moves for their Haka. It is still performed today either as historic re-enactment, or as a pre-game ritual for modern Polynesian sports teams.
